THE LINUX FOUNDATION

>> The Linux Foundation


The Linux Foundation is a non-profit organization dedicated to fostering the growth of Linux and collaborative software development. It was founded in 2000 by IBM, HP, NEC, and Intel. The Linux Foundation is a member-supported organization with more than 1,000 members, including individual developers, Fortune 500 companies, universities, non-profits, and government agencies. The Linux Foundation provides a neutral forum for collaboration and education by hosting Linux-related events such as LinuxCon and the Embedded Linux Conference, and by producing Linux-related training materials and publications.
